HOW TO PARTICIPATE IN THE CAMPAIGN

It's great that you want to get involved! Here are detailed step-by-step instructions describing what you can do to make your voice heard in this campaign. The whole process takes about five minutes.

  1. Find your local MP. The UK Parliament website has a useful resource which allows you to search for your MP by postcode. Once you've found your local MP, you can find their email address on their Parliament webpage.
  2. Use one of the letter templates to write an email to your MP. Now that you know who your MP is, write them an email to express your concern at the provisions of the Police, Crime, Courts and Sentencing Act, currently being debated in parliament. If you like, you can use one of the letter templates found on this site, but you're welcome to write your own. If you want more information about what sort of information to include in your letter, please refer to the Key Issues section.
  3. Let your MP know that you're writing as part of the Protect British Protest campaign. In the subject line of the email, write 'Urgent! Protect Our Right to Protest!'. If you're using one of the letter templates, you are encouraged to fill in the blanks with your name, your MP's name and your postcode in the appropriate places. If you don't want to include your name on the letter, then feel free to omit it.
  4. Send the email to your MP. That's it! Thanks again for participating in the letter writing campaign.

If you need help with any of the above steps or require assistance with anything else related to the campaign, please do not hesitate to contact me for assistance at protectbritishprotest@gmail.com. Thank you for your participation in the Protect British Protest campaign!
