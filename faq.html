FAQs

What is this campaign about?

Protect British Protest is a letter-writing campaign which encourages people living in the United Kingdom to contact their MP to vote against undemocratic provisions in the Police, Crime, Sentencing, and Courts Bill currently in progress in Parliament. The contested provisions seek to place excessive restrictions on both group and individual protests and introduce new, arbitrary offences which may result in lengthy imprisonment sentences. For a more detailed analysis of the issues this campaign is protesting, please look at the key issues page.

How can I get involved?

Write a letter to your MP! You can either use the letter templates on this website (we have seperate templates for England & Wales and for Scotland), or you can write your own letter. Detailed instructions, including how to find out who your MP is, can be found on the instructions page or if you want to learn more about the Police, Crime, Courts and Sentencing Bill we are campaigning against, check out the Key Issues page.

Who can participate in the campaign?

Anyone currently living in the United Kingdom may participate. All you need is an address in the UK. This includes non-UK citizens: so long as you live in the UK, you're allowed to participate.

What if my MP has already voted against the Bill?

It is still worthwhile to send a letter of protest to your MP because it voices new and potentially unconsidered perspectives on why the provisions in this Bill are so dangerous. This campaign has a dual goal; to urge MPs to vote against the Police, Crime, Sentencing and Courts Bill in its parliamentary journey and to encourage them to voice our concerns about the proposed restrictions on protests in parliamentary debate. We hope that by sending as many letters of protests to MPs as possible, a greater focus will be placed on protecting the right to protest from the provisions in the Bill.

Why does this campaign only targeting specific provisions in the Police, Crime, Sentencing & Courts Bill? Why not stand against the Bill as a whole?

The Police, Crime, Sentencing, and Courts Bill passed its second reading in Parliament on the 16th March 2021, with 359 votes for and 263 against. What this means is that the Bill as general concept has been approved by the majority of Parliament to pass into law. Unfortunately, this also means that it is a far harder task to ‘Kill the Bill’ completely at this stage in its parliamentary journey. What is still possible, however, is to advocate for the repeal or extensive amendment of the worst provisions in the Bill which will cause the most damage to the right to protest in the United Kingdom.

What will I achieve by writing a letter to my MP?

The aim of the letter of protest is to convince your MP to raise concerns about and vote against certain provisions in the Police, Crime, Sentencing, and Courts Bill which is currently in the approval process in Parliament. If enough MPs vocally oppose and vote against the contested provisions in the Bill, they will hopefully not get passed into British law.

Who do I send my letter to?

Letters of protest are to be sent to the MP who represents your area in Parliament. Your MP and their email address can be found by typing in your postcode on the UK Parliament website. Type in your postcode or the name of your constituency, and your MP will come up. Click on their name and you will be taken to their webpage, which lists contact information including their email address.

Is it OK to share this campaign on social media?

Please do! We want to raise as much awareness about this campaign as possble. That means sharing the campaign both in person and online. If you want to publicise this campain to a wider audience, please share on any social media with the hashtag #ProtectBritishProtest. Most MPs have twitter accounts, too, which you can find on their Parliament webpage. If you want to share the campaign on Twitter, write to your MP there and let them know you're participating!

I've received a response from my MP and I'm not sure whether to reply. What should I do?

It is a function of your MP to address their constituents’ concerns so when individuals send a letter to an MP, they will often receive a reply. Most of the time this response will contain details of your MP's position on the subject and the steps they are taking in dealing with the matter. It is usually not neccessary to repond to this but if you wish to reply to a communication from your MP regarding the campaign and don’t know how, please contact me and I will be more than happy to help.

Where can I find copies of the current law and proposed Bill?

The current law regulating protests is avaliable to view online or to download from the UK government legislation website. You can download and track the parliamentary progress of the Police, Crime, Sentencing and and Courts Bill on the UK parliament website.
