ABOUT THE CAMPAIGN

The Police, Crime, Courts & Sentencing Bill aims to curtail fundamental rights surrounding our right to peaceful protest.

The Bill gives police a broader authority to arrest protesters for behaviour that is difficult to quantify, like 'being too noisy', and to extend the maximum sentence for this manner of offence, from 3 months to a year. This letter-writing campaign aims to raise awareness about the undemocratic, draconian propositions of this bill, and to change the minds of MPs currently debating this bill in parliament.

ABOUT THE BILL

A large number of people have expressed their opposition to this bill, including in the form of petitions, demonstrations, peaceful protests, and campaigns such as this. Protect British Protest aims to work in tandem with these.

To learn more about what, in particular, this campaign is protesting against, go to the Key Issues section. Other campagins against the bill (as discussed below) have a similar focus.

OPPOSITION TO THE POLICE, CRIME, COURTS & SENTENCING BILL

A recent petition to the UK Parliament against the provisions in the Police, Courts & Sentencing Act recently gained support from huge populations across the UK. It now has over 250,000 signatures and, as such, was debated in Parliament on April 26, 2021. Caroline Lucas, as MP for Brighton Pavilion, spoke in this debate and provided a great explanation of what's wrong with the Bill, and why we need to fight it.

The activist group Amnesty International has organised a letter writing campaign of their own, where concerned citizens may write directly to the prime minister. We encourage you to participate in this campaign too.
